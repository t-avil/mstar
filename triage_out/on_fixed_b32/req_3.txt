Based on the visual characteristics of the dish, this is a popular street food known as **Snail Porridge** or **Snail Congee**.

Here are the key details that lead to this identification:

*   **The Snails:** The small, round, golden-brown items are cooked snails. They are typically prepared by boiling or steaming them until tender, then sautéing them with a rich, savory sauce.
*   **The Porridge Base:** The snails are served in a thick, savory porridge. The green flecks mixed into the porridge are most likely finely chopped **water spinach** (also known as kangkong or morning glory), a very common ingredient in this dish.
*   **The Presentation:** This dish is traditionally served in a large, round, metal bowl, which is often kept warm on a table. The entire bowl is then passed around the table for everyone to share.
