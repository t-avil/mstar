Based on the image provided, this is a classic Korean dish known as **Bibimbap**.

It is served in a traditional black stone bowl called a **dolsot**. Here's a breakdown of the ingredients visible in the bowl:

*   **Base:** A bed of white rice.
*   **Protein:** Thinly sliced meat, which appears to be beef, is mixed in with the rice.
*   **Vegetables:** A variety of colorful, seasoned vegetables (known as *namul*) are included. You can clearly see:
    *   Dark green leafy vegetables, likely seasoned spinach.
    *   Shredded carrots.
    *   Other vegetables like bean sprouts or zucchini.
*   **Egg:** A raw egg yolk is placed in the center, surrounded by the cooked egg white.
*   **Sauce:** A generous amount of red, savory sauce, which is almost certainly **gochujang** (Korean chili paste), is dolloped over the top of the ingredients.

The dish is typically served with a small amount of sesame oil and sesame seeds on top. The sizzling stone bowl keeps the ingredients hot and crispy, and the dish is usually eaten by mixing everything together with chopsticks
