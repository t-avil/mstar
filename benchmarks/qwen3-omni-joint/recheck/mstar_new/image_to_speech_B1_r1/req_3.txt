Based on the visual characteristics of the dish, this is **Egg Tarts** (also known as **Egg Custard Tarts**).

Here are the key features that lead to this identification:

*   **Individual Portion:** The dish consists of several small, individual tarts baked in a round metal pan.
*   **Crispy Shell:** Each tart has a golden-brown, flaky, and crisp-looking pastry shell.
*   **Creamy Filling:** The center is filled with a smooth, yellow custard made from egg yolks, milk, or cream.
*   **Browned Top:** The surface of the custard is lightly browned or caramelized, a classic characteristic of egg tarts.

This style of tart is very popular in Southern China, particularly in Guangdong province, where they are known as **蛋挞 (dàn tà)**.<|im_end|>
