Hello, this is a dish called **Escargot**.

More specifically, it's **Burgundy Escargot**. This is a classic French dish where edible snails, typically the *Helix pomatia* (Roman snail), are cooked and served in their shells.

They are usually prepared by:
*   **Sautéing:** The snails are first cooked in a butter and garlic mixture, often referred to as "escargot butter" or *aioli*.
*   **Serving in shells:** The cooked snails are placed back into their original shells.
*   **Baking:** They are then placed on a baking sheet and baked until the butter is bubbling and the shells are warm.
*   **Garnishing:** The dish is traditionally garnished with fresh parsley and lemon wedges, as seen in the image.

The snails are typically eaten with a special **escargot fork** to extract the meat from the shell.<|im_end|>
