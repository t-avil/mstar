Based on the visual evidence, this food is called **xiaolongbao** (or **xiaolongbao**).

Here are the key characteristics that lead to this identification:

*   **Steamed Dumplings:** They are cooked by steaming, which is evident from the condensation and steam on the parchment paper and the wooden steamer basket they are in.
*   **Delicate Skin:** The wrappers are very thin and translucent, which is a hallmark of xiaolongbao. This allows the shape of the filling and the broth inside to be partially visible.
*   **Pleated Top:** The dumplings have a distinct pleated or "crown-like" top, created by pinching the wrapper together. This is a classic feature of their preparation.
*   **Filling and Soup:** Xiaolongbao are famous for being filled with minced meat (usually pork) and a rich, savory broth. When steamed, the broth inside the dumpling turns into a liquid gel, creating a "soup dumpling" experience.

In short, the combination of the thin, steamed wrapper, the pleated top, and the plump, round shape are all defining features of xiaolongbao.<|im_end|>
