The James Webb Space Telescope, launched on Christmas Day 2021, represents one of the most ambitious scientific instruments ever built. Positioned nearly a million miles from Earth, it is designed to peer further back in time than any telescope before it, capturing light from galaxies that formed just a few hundred million years after the Big Bang. Scientists hope it will fundamentally reshape our understanding of the universe's origins, the formation of stars and planets, and perhaps even the conditions necessary for life.
